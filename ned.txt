According to the International Passenger Survey (IPS) research the Netherlands, with over 150 thousand tourists in 2012, is the fourth biggest market for Scottish tourism. Guests from this country stayed in Scotland for 946 thousand nights and spent 77 million pounds. In the next ten years the number of Dutch tourists coming to Scotland is expected to grow steadily.
The Netherlands is a market with great potential, as the Dutch consider holiday trips as a very important part of life and as many as 81% of them were planning to go on holiday trip in 2013, of which more than a half were going to spend their holiday abroad.







Although the Dutch are believed to plan and book their trips at the last moment, the research conducted by VisitScotland.org shows the opposite - the Dutch begin to plan their holidays earlier than the other European nations. They start planning their holiday on average 20 weeks earlier and books accommodation and transport 13 weeks in advance.

When planning holiday, the Dutch usually use online searching, travel brochures and articles in a newspaper or magazine. The most popular form of booking holidays is by internet travel agencies, but recently more and more Dutch book directly on the websites of accommodation and transport providers.
In comparison to the other European nations, the Dutch often avoid to book accommodation in advance (41%, compared with 24% of the Europe average). The reason is that the Dutch, unlike the tourists from other country, like to stay at camping or hostels that don't need to be booked earlier. Besides, they have great interest in car tours around a country without a route planned in advance.


The most popular types of holidays among the Dutch tourists are car tours around a country with sightseeing (81%) and short break visits to a selected city (50%). 
Recently also themed holidays become more and more popular (i.e. cycling or hiking holidays). Young Dutch tourists are keen on active and adventurous holidays.

The key factors the Dutch take into account when selecting their holiday destination are rest, recreation, sun & bath and nature. When asked about their motivation for travelling abroad, they say they want to learn about new countries, experience their way of life, discover new places and explore beautiful landscapes. If they decide to try a new holiday destination, they are usually inspired by an article they had read in a newspaper or magazine (74%), a really great opportunity they had found (35%) or a movie or TV program they had watched (34%).
